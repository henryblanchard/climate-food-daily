## 1. FMC Sells India Crop Protection Business to Crystal for $252 Million

US agrichemical group FMC Corporation has agreed to sell its India commercial operations to Crystal Crop Protection Limited for $252 million, expected to close by end-2026 subject to regulatory approval. FMC will direct all proceeds toward debt reduction. Crystal gains a licence to FMC's India brands and preferred access to its pipeline of active ingredients; FMC retains R&D and manufacturing presence in the country. The deal is part of a broader FMC strategy to concentrate resources on its highest-growth global markets.

*Why this matters:* Domestic consolidators are absorbing multinational crop-protection assets across emerging markets, a trend operators and input distributors in India should watch closely for pricing and supply-chain implications.

## 2. Corteva Names Its Seed Spinout Vylor, Targets Q4 2026 Separation

Corteva has confirmed its seeds business will be called Vylor when it separates from the crop chemicals unit in late 2026. The split creates two independently listed pure-plays: New Corteva for crop protection and Vylor for seeds. Analysts are focusing on how the two companies will divide shared AI platforms and proprietary agronomic data, which underpins much of both businesses' competitive positioning.

*Why this matters:* Splitting seeds from chemicals sharpens the investment thesis in each category and could trigger deal activity, as buyers with different strategic rationales can now target either asset cleanly.

## 3. MiAlgae's Whisky-Waste Omega-3 Facility Enters Production at Grangemouth

Scottish biotech MiAlgae's new Grangemouth facility, built to grow microalgae on whisky distillery by-products and extract DHA omega-3 oil, was slated to come online in early Q2 2026 following a groundbreaking backed by up to £3 million in joint UK and Scottish government funding. The site was designed to increase MiAlgae's omega-3 output more than tenfold, replacing marine-sourced omega-3s equivalent to around six billion fish annually while recycling over 36 million litres of whisky co-products each year. MiAlgae's circular feedstock model positions Grangemouth as a low-carbon supply point for pet nutrition and aquaculture customers across the UK, EU, and US.

*Why this matters:* Fish-free omega-3 supply is a structural bottleneck for sustainable aquaculture feed; government-backed scale-up like this lowers the cost curve and de-risks adoption for feed manufacturers.

[Source: MiAlgae, "MiAlgae Breaks Ground on Major Sustainable Algal Omega 3 Facility at Grangemouth"](https://mialgae.com/news/mialgae-breaks-ground-on-major-sustainable-algal-omega-3-facility-at-grangemouth/)

## 4. Agreena Issues 2.3 Million Verified Carbon Credits Across European Arable Farms

Danish agri-fintech Agreena has completed verification of 2.3 million Verified Carbon Units under Verra's VCS methodology (VM0042 Improved Agricultural Land Management), becoming the first large-scale arable farming programme verified under that standard. Running across more than 4.5 million hectares and 2,300 farmers in 20 European countries, Agreena operates a dual-pathway model issuing both Verra VCS credits and SustainCERT Verified Impact Units to accommodate different corporate climate reporting frameworks. Satellite-based monitoring and reporting underpins the verification process, cutting per-hectare measurement costs as the programme expands.

*Why this matters:* Dual-standard certification and satellite MRV are the two ingredients most needed to make agricultural soil carbon credible for institutional corporate buyers, and Agreena is now demonstrating both at scale.

[Source: Carbon Credits, "Scaling Sustainable Farming: AgreenaCarbon's 2.3 Million Verified Carbon Credits Redefine Regenerative Agriculture"](https://carboncredits.com/scaling-sustainable-farming-agreenacarbons-2-3-million-verified-carbon-credits-redefine-regenerative-agriculture/)

## 5. Ferrero, Mars, and Four Other Food Companies Back Regenerative Peanut Programme in Argentina

Soil Capital has launched a three-year regenerative agriculture initiative in Argentina's Cordoba province covering peanut supply chains, co-funded by six SAI Platform members: Ferrero, Importaco, Intersnack, Lorenz, Mars, and Pip and Nut. Argentina is the world's leading peanut exporter and a major sourcing geography for European confectionery and snack brands. Field implementation began in March 2026, with local partners GapGreen and Plataforma Puma supporting growers in adopting practices that improve soil health, build climate resilience, and generate credible Scope 3 emissions data for buyers.

*Why this matters:* Collective corporate funding at supply-shed level, rather than bilateral deals, is the model most likely to shift practice at scale and is increasingly the standard demanded by corporate net-zero frameworks.

[Source: Soil Capital, "Soil Capital announces landscape partnership in Argentina to deliver Scope 3 outcomes in peanut supply chains"](https://www.soilcapital.com/news/soil-capital-announces-landscape-partnership-in-argentina-to-deliver-scope-3-outcomes-in-peanut-supply-chains)

## 6. Gourmey's Cultivated Duck Application Fast-Tracked for Review in Australia

French cultivated meat startup Gourmey has had its application to Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ) accepted and classified as a General Level 5 procedure, requiring only a single public consultation. FSANZ expects to complete its review by late August 2026. If approved, Gourmey's cultivated duck, grown from embryonic Pekin duck stem cells, would be used as an ingredient at between 5% and 80% inclusion by weight in finished products such as pate. Gourmey is the first international company to seek regulatory approval in Australia for a cultivated meat product.

*Why this matters:* Australia is proving a faster regulatory pathway for cultivated meat than Europe or North America, giving companies a commercial reference approval that can be cited in other jurisdictions.

[Source: Cultivated X, "Gourmey Submits Application to Food Standards Australia New Zealand for Approval of Cultivated Duck"](https://cultivated-x.com/approvals/gourmey-application-food-standards-australia-new-zealand-regulatory-approval-cultivated-duck/)
