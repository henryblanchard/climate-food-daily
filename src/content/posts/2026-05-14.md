## 1. Gates Foundation and UK FCDO Back New Global Wheat Health Alliance

CIMMYT and Cornell University have launched the Global Wheat Health Alliance (GWHA) at CIMMYT's headquarters in Texcoco, Mexico, with $2.7 million in funding from the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation and the UK's Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office. The alliance, a workstream within the broader HyBread initiative on disease-resistant hybrid wheat, will generate large-scale disease phenotyping data, deliver wheat lines with stacked resistance to multiple fungal pathogens including stem rust, yellow rust, wheat blast and Fusarium head blight, and train at least 100 scientists over three years. The John Innes Centre and the University of Maryland join CIMMYT and Cornell in contributing gene discovery, mapping and gene editing expertise, with a focus on farming communities in South Asia and East Africa.

*Why this matters:* Stacked disease resistance in widely grown wheat varieties could reduce fungicide dependency at scale and stabilise yields under the shifting pathogen pressure that comes with climate change, making this a food security intervention with a direct emissions co-benefit.

[Source: CIMMYT, "Global Wheat Health Alliance Launched to Strengthen Disease Resistance in Wheat"](https://www.cimmyt.org/news/global-wheat-health-alliance-launched-to-strengthen-disease-resistance-in-wheat/)

## 2. Germany's Muller Dairy Acquires 40-Year-Old Plant-Based Pioneer Berief Food

Theo Muller Group, Germany's second-largest dairy company, has announced plans to acquire Berief Food, a family-owned plant-based milk, yogurt and tofu producer that has been operating out of Beckum since 1985. The deal, announced May 7 and subject to regulatory clearance, will see Berief continue as an independent business unit; Muller said it gains "an attractive product range, additional expertise, and production capacities" in the growing plant-based segment. Berief's founders plan to remain involved during a transition period, and Muller is targeting accelerated expansion into the Benelux region and Eastern Europe.

*Why this matters:* European dairy incumbents building plant-based positions through acquisition rather than greenfield brands signals a structural confidence in the category's long-term volume growth, and consolidation typically compresses the cost of capital available to standalone plant-based producers.

[Source: Green Queen Media, "Germany's Muller to Acquire Plant-Based Dairy Pioneer Berief Food"](https://www.greenqueen.com.hk/theo-muller-group-berief-food-plant-based-milk-dairy-acquisition/)

## 3. Swiss Startup Planetary Raises $28M to License Its Fermentation Platform to Industry

Planetary, a Swiss biotech building mycoprotein from sugar-milling co-products, closed a CHF 22 million ($28 million) round in late April, combining CHF 16 million in equity led by Radikal Capital and Oetker Ventures with CHF 6 million in credit. The round brings total funding to roughly $40 million. Planetary co-locates production with Schweizer Zucker's sugar mill in Frauenfeld, converting low-value molasses into food-grade mycoprotein, and plans to license its "BioBlocks" fermentation platform to agrifood corporates globally. Strategic investors Royal Cosun, Astanor Ventures and XAnge also participated.

*Why this matters:* A dual-revenue model combining B2B ingredient sales with platform licensing is a more capital-efficient path to scale than building dedicated fermentation factories, and participation by Royal Cosun, one of Europe's largest sugar cooperatives, points to the adjacent opportunity in valorising agricultural waste streams.

[Source: AgFunderNews, "Planetary nets $28m to scale fermentation infrastructure and licensing platform"](https://agfundernews.com/planetary-nets-28m-to-scale-fermentation-infrastructure-and-licensing-platform)

## 4. Virtual Cattle Fencing Startup Halter Raises $220M at $2B Valuation

New Zealand agtech company Halter closed a $220 million Series E in late March, led by Founders Fund at a valuation just above $2 billion. Blackbird, DCVC, Bond and Bessemer also participated. Halter's solar-powered collar combines GPS positioning with a network of low-frequency towers to allow farmers to create virtual fences, move herds remotely, and monitor every animal around the clock via a smartphone app. The collar is now deployed on more than one million cattle across more than 2,000 farms in New Zealand, Australia and 22 US states, with controlled rotational grazing lifting land productivity by as much as 20%, according to the company.

*Why this matters:* Founders Fund's bet at a $2 billion valuation validates connected livestock as a category-defining infrastructure play rather than a niche tool; the precision grazing data Halter generates also positions it as a credible measurement layer for rotational grazing carbon-credit programs.

[Source: BusinessWire, "Halter Raises $220M in Series E to Accelerate Global Expansion of Virtual Fencing"](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260324036414/en/Halter-Raises-$220M-in-Series-E-to-Accelerate-Global-Expansion-of-Virtual-Fencing)

## 5. London's Biographica Raises $9.5M to Speed Up AI-Designed Climate-Resilient Crops

Biographica, a London-based machine learning and genomics startup founded in 2022, raised a £7 million ($9.5 million) seed round in January, led by Faber VC with participation from SuperSeed, Cardumen Capital, the EQT Foundation and Sie Ventures. The company uses AI models trained on genomic and phenotypic data to identify gene-editing targets for traits including drought tolerance, disease resistance and climate resilience. In pilots with seed companies, the platform found proven gene targets 12 times faster than traditional breeding workflows. Alongside the round, Biographica disclosed commercial contracts with BASF Nunhems and Cibus tied to development milestones.

*Why this matters:* A milestone-linked commercial contract with the world's largest vegetable seed company at seed stage is an unusually early validation signal, suggesting Biographica's platform is generating actionable breeding intelligence rather than just a faster literature review.

[Source: AgFunderNews, "Biographica raises $9.5m for AI-driven crop design, unveils partnership with BASF"](https://agfundernews.com/exclusive-biographica-raises-9-5m-for-ai-driven-crop-design-unveils-partnership-with-basf)

## 6. Ruminant BioTech Closes NZD17M to Bring Its Methane Bolus to Pasture Cattle

New Zealand startup Ruminant BioTech raised NZD17 million (around $9.5 million USD) in a Series A co-led by Rosrain Investments and Cultivate Ventures, with further investment from AgriZeroNZ and Marex. The capital funds commercialisation of its slow-release bolus, which steadily delivers a methane inhibitor in the rumen over an extended period. Animal trials show a single dose can cut methane output by roughly 75% for more than 100 days, with the team targeting six-month efficacy over time. Regulatory approval is being sought first in New Zealand, then Canada, Brazil and the EU.

*Why this matters:* Approaches such as Bovaer require daily top-dressing of feed and are suited to intensive feedlots; a long-acting bolus that works for pasture-raised animals addresses the harder and larger problem, since the majority of the world's 1 billion beef cattle are grass-fed rather than grain-fed.
