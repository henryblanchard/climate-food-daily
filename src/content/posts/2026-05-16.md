## 1. Oishii Closes $150M Series C as Premium Strategy Separates It from the Vertical Farming Wreckage

Japan-founded, US-based indoor berry grower Oishii has announced the first closing of a $150 million Series C, led by SPARX Asset Management with participation from Nomura Real Estate Development, MISUMI Group, and Mizuho Bank, bringing total funding to roughly $370 million. The raise comes as many of its peers have filed for bankruptcy over the past three years, with high energy costs and thin margins proving fatal to first-wave indoor farming companies. Oishii's approach has been deliberately different: rather than chasing volume, it launched its Omakase Berry at close to $50 a tray, built proprietary robotics for pollination and harvesting (including through the 2025 acquisition of Tortuga AgTech), and kept capital intensity disciplined. The new capital will fund US and Japan farm infrastructure expansion and continued R&D.

*Why this matters:* Pricing power and disciplined scaling, not early volume, may be the only financially viable model for indoor farming, and a $150 million raise in this environment is the clearest proof point yet.

## 2. EU Council Formally Adopts Two-Tier Gene-Editing Framework for Crops

The EU Council adopted the New Genomic Techniques (NGT) regulation on April 21, creating a two-category system for plants developed through precision gene editing. Category 1 plants, deemed equivalent to varieties that could arise through conventional breeding, are exempt from GMO authorisation and mandatory consumer labelling; Category 2 plants, with more substantial modifications, remain under full GMO rules including traceability and authorisation. A patent transparency measure requires Category 1 developers to submit IP information to a public database, a concession won by farmer groups concerned about seed industry concentration. The European Parliament still needs to formally sign off, and the rules will not apply until mid-2028, but the Council vote is the decisive political moment after years of deadlock.

*Why this matters:* This is the most significant shift in EU agbiotech policy in a generation, opening a commercial pathway for drought-tolerant and disease-resistant crop varieties that seed companies and investors have long waited for.

[Source: Council of the EU, "New genomic techniques: Council adopts new rules to boost sustainable and competitive EU food systems"](https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2026/04/21/new-genomic-techniques-council-adopts-new-rules-to-boost-sustainable-and-competitive-eu-food-systems/)

## 3. Philippines Launches $1 Billion World Bank Program for Climate-Resilient Farm Modernisation

The Philippine Department of Agriculture launched the Philippine Sustainable Agriculture Transformation (PSAT) program on May 15, the country's first project under the World Bank's Program-for-Results financing model, which releases funds only when verified results are delivered. The $1 billion World Bank loan is paired with a $24.5 million technical assistance grant, including $14.5 million from the UK's Just Rural Transition Support Program. PSAT targets at least five million farmers, aiming to diversify production into vegetables, fruits, livestock, and aquaculture, modernise cold-chain logistics, and deploy a new digital voucher system for farm inputs.

*Why this matters:* The Program-for-Results structure, where disbursement is tied to measured outcomes rather than inputs, is becoming the preferred vehicle for agricultural transformation at scale; this is one of the largest deployments of that model in the Asia-Pacific food sector.

[Source: Manila Times, "World Bank, UK launch $1B agri program for PH"](https://www.manilatimes.net/2026/05/16/business/top-business/world-bank-uk-launch-1b-agri-program-for-ph/2345128)

## 4. World Bank-Hosted GAFSP Opens $163M Grant Round for Smallholder Farming in Poorest Countries

The Global Agriculture and Food Security Program (GAFSP), hosted at the World Bank, opened a $163 million grant call for proposals on May 13, targeting governments in IDA-eligible countries, the World Bank's bracket for its poorest member states. Applications close September 15 and awards are expected in January 2027. The call lands as 2025 data puts 266 million people across 47 countries facing high levels of acute food insecurity, described by the fund as the second-highest severity on record. Since 2010, GAFSP has distributed more than $2.46 billion across 53 low-income countries, focusing on climate resilience and smallholder productivity.

*Why this matters:* For agtech companies building solutions for smallholder farmers in fragile markets, GAFSP grants are a meaningful public co-financing lever that can de-risk commercial pilots and create the credibility needed to attract follow-on private capital.

[Source: AgTech Navigator, "World Bank-backed fund launches $163m push as 'perfect storm' fuels global hunger"](https://www.agtechnavigator.com/Article/2026/05/15/world-bank-backed-fund-launches-163m-push-as-perfect-storm-fuels-global-hunger/)

## 5. Corteva Names Its Seed and Genetics Spinout "Vylor"

Corteva, one of the world's largest seed and crop protection companies, named its planned seed and genetics spinout Vylor, Inc. on May 4, after referring to it only as "SpinCo" since the separation was announced in October 2025. Vylor will inherit the largest commercial seed production network globally, market-leading positions in corn and soybean in almost every region it serves, more than 4,000 germplasm patents, and 2,000 biotechnology patents. Hybrid wheat, gene editing capabilities, and multi-disease-resistant corn varieties are among the pipeline priorities flagged at launch. The split from Corteva's crop protection business is targeted for Q4 2026, creating two separately listed public companies.

*Why this matters:* A pure-play seed and genetics company with Vylor's patent depth and market breadth will be a more focused acquirer of emerging agbiotech platforms and a cleaner investment vehicle, potentially reshaping exit dynamics for gene-editing startups across Europe and North America.

[Source: PR Newswire, "Introducing Vylor: Corteva Spinoff to Propel Agriculture Further"](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/introducing-vylor-corteva-spinoff-to-propel-agriculture-further-302760803.html)
