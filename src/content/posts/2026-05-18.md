## 1. S2G Investments Closes $1 Billion Fund for the Climate-Food "Missing Middle"

S2G Investments, the Chicago-based fund backed by Walmart heir Lukas Walton, has closed Solutions Fund I at $1 billion, targeting growth-stage companies in food and agriculture, energy, and oceans. The firm describes its focus as the "missing middle": companies that have outgrown seed and venture but cannot yet access infrastructure capital. With $300 million already deployed across ten investments and $2.8 billion in assets under management, S2G secured commitments from pension funds, funds of funds, and family offices across North America, Europe, Asia, and Australia. The fund's first exit came through Urbint, an AI-enabled utility risk platform acquired by Itron.

*Why this matters:* A $1 billion close targeting the financing gap that has stalled the most commercially mature climate-food companies is the clearest signal yet that institutional capital is moving back into the sector at scale.

## 2. Oishii Closes $150 Million Series C on a Premium Vertical Farming Thesis

Oishii, the Japanese-American controlled-environment agriculture company, announced a first close of $150 million in Series C financing on May 13, led by SPARX Asset Management with participation from Nomura Real Estate Development, MISUMI Group, and Mizuho Bank. The raise brings total funding to $370 million since 2016. Capital will expand production capacity and robotics integration across the US and Japan. Oishii's differentiation has been deliberate: rather than competing on commodity crops, it launched the Omakase Berry at close to $50 per tray, building pricing power before chasing volume.

*Why this matters:* Where most vertical farming peers collapsed chasing commodity economics, Oishii's premium-first approach and Japanese institutional backing suggests that CEA can generate returns, but only for operators willing to resist the pressure to scale before achieving margin.

[Source: AgTech Navigator, "Oishii secures $150m Series C as premium strategy sets it apart from vertical farming failures"](https://www.agtechnavigator.com/Article/2026/05/14/oishii-secures-150m-series-c-as-premium-strategy-sets-it-apart-from-vertical-farming-failures/)

## 3. UK Startup Adamo Foods Wins EUR 10 Million EU Grant to Scale Mycelium Steak

UK food-tech company Adamo Foods has secured a share of a EUR 10 million Horizon Europe grant through the Circular Bio-based Europe Joint Undertaking, under the MycoStruct project spanning 12 food, biotech, and research organisations. The three-year programme will scale Adamo's fungal fermentation platform, which uses triple-patented technology to recreate the fibrous texture of animal muscle tissue with a five-ingredient formulation. The company claims the process generates 93% fewer greenhouse gas emissions than conventional beef, and it is targeting UK retail availability and price parity with beef by 2027.

*Why this matters:* EU public capital flowing into whole-cut mycelium production, combined with an aggressive price-parity timeline, positions Adamo as the company most likely to test whether consumers will accept fungi-based whole cuts on price alone.

[Source: FoodBev Media, "Adamo Foods lands EUR 10m EU-backed funding to scale whole-cut Mycelium steak production"](https://www.foodbev.com/news/adamo-foods-lands-10m-eu-grant-to-scale-whole-cut-mycelium-steak-production)

## 4. Resurrect Bio Finalises $10.3 Million Series A to Restore Crop Immunity with AI

Resurrect Bio, a Norwich-based ag-biotech spun out of The Sainsbury Laboratory, closed its Series A at $10.3 million, adding $2.2 million to an initial $8.1 million close announced in February. The round was led by Corteva Catalyst, with participation from Calculus Capital, Future Planet Capital, and AgFunder. The company's platform uses an AI-powered protein prediction tool called FloraFold to identify how to restore the plant immune responses that pathogens have learned to suppress. A licensing partnership with Corteva on corn disease resistance was signed in March.

*Why this matters:* Disease resistance encoded into the plant itself rather than sprayed as a chemical input is a structural cost and emissions reduction for growers; Corteva's co-investment signals the major input players are treating AI-led crop immunity as a credible near-term pipeline.

[Source: AgFunderNews, "Resurrect Bio expands Series A to $10.3m to scale tech helping plants to defend themselves"](https://agfundernews.com/resurrect-bio-expands-series-a-to-10-3m-to-scale-tech-helping-plants-to-defend-themselves)

## 5. Amazon Signs $30 Million Carbon Credit Deal with Indian Rice Farmers

Amazon has committed $30 million to purchase more than 685,000 metric tonnes of CO2-equivalent carbon credits from India's Good Rice Alliance, a programme primarily backed by Bayer and run in collaboration with GenZero and Shell. The credits will be generated by 13,000 smallholder farmers cultivating 35,000 hectares across India, using climate-smart techniques including improved water management and adjusted nutrient practices that studies show can cut paddy methane by 30 to 50%. Rice cultivation accounts for approximately 8-10% of global methane emissions, the second-largest agricultural methane source after livestock.

*Why this matters:* A $30 million corporate purchase commitment is the demand anchor that voluntary agricultural carbon markets in South Asia have been waiting for; it validates cropland methane reduction at scale as a legitimate corporate offset category.

[Source: ESG Today, "Amazon Signs 685,000 Ton Carbon Credit Deal to Reduce Emissions from Rice Farming in India"](https://www.esgtoday.com/amazon-signs-685000-ton-carbon-credit-deal-to-reduce-emissions-from-rice-farming-in-india/)
